About Yamaha

n the 1970s, Yamaha gained significant recognition for its innovative motorcycles, particularly the RD series, which became popular for their speed and handling. During the 1980s, Yamaha expanded its influence in the music industry by introducing the groundbreaking DX7 synthesizer, revolutionizing electronic music production. The company also made strides in motorsports, achieving numerous victories in events like the MotoGP, further solidifying its reputation in both the motorcycle and music industries.
RD - Series

The Yamaha RD series, released in the 1970s, became iconic for its lightweight design and powerful two-stroke engines. Known for its agility, the RD gained a strong following among motorcycle enthusiasts. The RD350, in particular, was celebrated for its performance and speed on both roads and tracks.
Milestones

The Yamaha RD series, launched in 1973 with the RD-350, quickly established itself as a leader in the performance motorcycle market, known for its powerful two-stroke engines and lightweight design. The series expanded in 1976 with the introduction of the RD-400. In 1984, Yamaha released the RD-500, a V4-powered bike inspired by Grand Prix racing, which became a legend among sportbike enthusiasts. Throughout the 1970s and 1980s, the RD series achieved international success and cult status, particularly in racing circuits, until stricter emissions regulations and the rise of four-stroke engines led to the end of its production in the late 1980s.
